# Foundational Philosophy: Organization

Let’s touch on the question of what philosophy really is. To systemize it better, we must define it rigorously.

You can say it’s systems of thought, and its study, critique, collection, and organization. But what if we think of it differently? A definition where branches can grow out of it naturally.

Philosophy is a systemic study of fundamental abstract ideas in which there are different fields and subfields, and how we study, analyse, and organize it using arguments.

Now we should critique and study this definition to find out what it really means: how, why, where, and when branches grow out of it.

Is this definition always true? In the cases we are covering, yes, it is.

“Fundamental abstract ideas” unpacking this gives us: a few core key beliefs which make more complex beliefs thoroughly.

Analysing its branches, it’s clear fields will naturally come out of the philosophical system. We can organize it as axes (x, y).

> “To seek knowledge, we must first doubt all that can be doubted, then build a structure of truth from indubitable foundations.”  
> — René Descartes, *Meditations*

---

# X Slider (Scope of Application)

**Collective (10) <-> Individual (0)**

In this case, “Collective” means any philosophy that doesn’t affect a single person; it affects multiple people systematically under a code.

Examples:

- Communism
- Capitalism
- Socialism
- Populism

I treat economic and any system that affects mass amounts of people as political.

In this axis, “Individual” on the right means anything that determines your personal way of life and philosophy of mind.

Examples:

- Nihilism
- Stoicism
- Romanticism
- Absurdism

I treat anything existential or anything that affects the individual scale as personal.

This system roughly defines our foundation for graphing philosophies.

---

# Y Slider (Human Nature)

**Realist (10)**  
⬆️  
⬇️  
**Idealist (0)**

I am not using these in the Kant/Hegel sense, but more so in a general sense.

In this case, “Realist” philosophies are the ones which account for the human psyche and take a thorough approach to the real world.

Examples:

- Utilitarianism
- Machiavellianism
- Hobbesian Philosophy
- Mixed Socialism

I treat moral outcomes and philosopher beliefs under this as well.

In this axis, “Idealist” means philosophies which believe humans are inherently **not immoral** and are morally perfect.

Examples:

- Ideal Utopian Communism
- Pure Anarchy
- Absolute Self-Perfectionism
- Absolute Libertarianism

For this metric, types of these can work, but the standard for not being idealist is taking what humans would naturally do.

I graph both personal and political philosophies under both of these axes.

This framework accounts for ethical, epistemological, logical, and part metaphysical philosophies, so it is well-rounded.

Existential will be covered later and thoroughly because it doesn’t quite fit in this framework, but rather as a slider.
