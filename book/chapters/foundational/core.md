This is the core part of the chapter. In this section, we will make it even more rigorous, expand the definition, as well as critique it.

The framework we have built can be flexible and applied to different angles of different philosophies to study them deeply and conceptually. In this chapter, I will include more rigorous definitions of what “belief” and “argument” really mean in our system.

A **belief** is a proposition held to be true by an individual or system. It is backed by arguments to justify its truthfulness.

An **argument** is a way to prove how accurate and thorough a belief is. It is a structured justification using logic, evidence, or principles to support a belief.

Usually, a well-crafted argument uses key axioms of logic and ethics.

# Philosophy Fields

## Political Philosophy

Political Philosophy is where a system of ideology controls a group of people under one system, whether it is economic, ideological, or collective.

## Moral Philosophy

Moral Philosophy is where morality, ethics, and their outcomes are studied and used. It asks what is right, wrong, or in between.

## Anthropological Philosophy

Anthropological Philosophy is how human development should work, what we need to do, and what is cosmologically wrong or right. It is similar but different to Moral Philosophy.

## Existential Philosophy

Existential Philosophy is how deep questions, especially about epistemology, cosmology, and metaphysics, are studied and thought out.

## Sociological Philosophy

Sociological Philosophy is how humans should act toward other humans, but not politically, for an individual reason or purpose. It is also individual, but affects other people based on what the individual does.

## Analytical Philosophy

Analytical Philosophy is how we treat knowledge, logic, and structure itself. It is mostly epistemological and logical.

## Aesthetical Philosophy

Aesthetical Philosophy is not just beauty. It studies why we perceive beauty as pleasure, what pleasure is, what consciousness even is, and the structure behind meaning. However, it is not existential; rather, it studies how existential ideas are perceived.

# Statement Types

Another thing in philosophy you might not have seen is what type of statement it creates.

They are:

- **Questioning** — e.g. Existential, Moral
- **Interpreting** — e.g. Analytical, Anthropological
- **Answering** — e.g. Political, Sociological

**Questioning** are the ones that simply question, where there may be no answer for the question at hand.

**Interpreting** are the ones that interpret a question, such as what question it is, what question it brings out, and similar concepts.

**Answering** are the ones that answer the question. They tell you what to do and provide the answer to the question, which is objective within their systems.

These are called **Statement Types**, but they can only be applied as tags/fields. You can say Machiavellianism is Answering, then find its family of tags, which are Sociological and Moral, and see what statement types those tags follow.

# Statement Type Structure

This is the list of what statement type tags follow:

| Field | Statement Type |
|---|---|
| Sociological | Answering |
| Anthropological | Interpreting |
| Individual | Answering |
| Existential | Questioning |
| Analytical | Interpreting |
| Moral | Answering |
| Aesthetical | Questioning |
| Political | Answering |

A useful feature is that when a philosophy has multiple tags, it inherits a new structure of statement types.

For example, Nihilism is arguably both Individual and Existential. From our list above, Existential is Questioning and Individual is Answering.

We can handle this by creating a format for Nihilism. A format is the structure in which a philosophy resides if two statement types of its tags contradict each other.

First, let’s define what happens when statement types mix:

- **Questioning + Answering:** Prescriptive Doubt
- **Questioning + Interpreting:** Reflective Inquiry
- **Interpreting + Answering:** Applied Prescription
- **Questioning + Interpreting + Answering:** Total System

First of all, it is rare for philosophies to have a pure statement type from their tags which do not contradict each other, but it is not impossible.
