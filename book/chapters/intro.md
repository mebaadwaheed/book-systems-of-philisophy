# Intro

The world of philosophy is very interesting. It is not a single central umbrella of smaller beliefs, but rather the study of systems of thought, including collections of different doctrines: political, realist, idealist, personal, existential, and so on.

What even is philosophy? This is also a philosophical question. No matter what definition you give it, at its core it is a system of ideas and beliefs which all require abstract thinking.

While most individual philosophies may emphasize different arguments, philosophy usually touches on:

- Logic
- Ethics
- Metaphysics
- Aesthetics
- Epistemology

While I won’t go too in-depth into these separate branches, you should know that most philosophies touch on them.

My goal for this is to give you an accurate representation of what philosophy really is and how to organize it cleanly and systematically.

You can take what I say with a grain of salt, because I am not an expert on it. I just like to sometimes explore the structure and integrity of systems and arguments.

This is not a canonical classification. It is an open methodology. Apply it yourself, add your own philosophies, and the system will produce different maps for different users.

The framework is the constant, but the scores are invitations to argue.
